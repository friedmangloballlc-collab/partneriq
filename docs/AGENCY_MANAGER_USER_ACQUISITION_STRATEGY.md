### Channel 7: Referral Program — Agency to Agency

**Program Structure: DealStage Agency Advocate Program**

**Incentive: Cash, Not Just Credits**

- Referring agency earns $500 cash per paying agency referred (Starter tier)
- $1,000 cash per Pro tier referral
- $2,500 cash per Enterprise referral

**Why Cash Over Credits**
Agencies operate on tight margins and value cash over platform credits. Offering cash demonstrates respect for the agency's referral effort and creates a stronger incentive to actively recommend rather than passively mention.

**How to Activate Referrals**

- Prompt inside the platform: "Know another agency that would benefit from DealStage? Earn $500-$2,500 per referral."
- Quarterly email to active agency accounts: "Your referral link" + reminder of incentive
- Customer success check-ins at 30/60/90 days: "Who else in your network runs an agency with a similar challenge?"

**Referral Mechanics**

- Unique referral link per agency account
- Referred agency gets 20% off first 3 months (adds incentive to act on recommendation)
- Referring agency dashboard shows referral status and pending payouts
- Minimum payout threshold: $500 (encourages multiple referrals before first payout)

**Agency Referral Ecosystem Targets**

- Sports agencies refer sister agencies in other sports verticals
- Influencer agencies in different geographic markets (LA agency refers NYC agency)
- Agencies that serve complementary talent categories (music agency refers sports agency)

## 1.3 Enterprise Sales Playbook — Agency

### Identifying Decision Makers in Large Agencies

**The Buying Committee in a 50+ Person Agency**

| Stakeholder                      | Role in Decision             | Pain Point                             | Demo Focus                                     |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| CEO / Founding Partner           | Final approval, budget       | "Are we using our team's time well?"   | ROI, time savings, competitive differentiation |
| COO / Head of Operations         | Process owner                | "Our workflows are broken"             | Approval workflows, cross-client analytics     |
| Head of Partnerships             | Primary user                 | "I need better deal visibility"        | Deal pipeline, AI Match Engine                 |
| CFO / Finance Lead               | Budget gatekeeper            | "What does this cost vs what we save?" | Cost-benefit, admin hours eliminated           |
| IT / Tech Lead (larger agencies) | Security/integration concern | "Does this integrate with our stack?"  | API capabilities, SSO, data security           |

**Identifying the Economic Buyer**

- In agencies under 50 people: CEO or Founding Partner is both champion and economic buyer
- In agencies 50-200 people: COO or Head of Operations is champion; CEO approves
- In agencies 200+ people: Dedicated procurement process — need internal champion at VP level

**Finding Decision Makers**

- LinkedIn: Company page + employee list, filter by title
- Crunchbase: Org structure clues for funded agencies
- Agency website "Our Team" page
- Industry event speaker lists
- Podcast appearances (talent agency leaders are active on business podcasts)

---

### Multi-Stakeholder Demo Strategy

**Phase 1: Champion Demo (30 minutes)**

- Audience: Head of Partnerships or Director of BD
- Goal: Make them the internal advocate
- Show: Their day-to-day pain points solved (deal pipeline, outreach, approvals)
- End with: "Would this change how your team operates?" — if yes, request executive demo

**Phase 2: Executive Demo (45 minutes)**

- Audience: CEO + COO (or equivalent)
- Goal: Business case approval
- Show: ROI calculator (hours saved x team size x billing rate), cross-client analytics, competitive advantage narrative
- Include: Reference to similar agency using the platform (if available)
- End with: "What would need to be true for this to be a priority this quarter?"

**Phase 3: Technical/Security Review (30 minutes)**

- Audience: IT lead, CTO (if exists)
- Goal: Remove technical blockers
- Cover: Data security (SOC 2 compliance if applicable), API documentation, SSO/SAML options, data export capabilities, SLA terms
- Provide: Security one-pager, data processing agreement template

**Phase 4: Commercial Negotiation**

- Audience: COO + Finance lead
- Provide: Three pricing options (good/better/best structure)
- Include: Multi-year discount (10% Year 2, 15% Year 3)
- Offer: Phased rollout option (start with 1 team/department, expand)

- Brands actively searching for talent on DealStage creates inbound demand

- As brand roster grows, talent acquisition becomes a demand-pull rather than push

**Flywheel Mechanism 3: Agencies as Batch Acquisition Engines**

- One agency signing = 10-50 talent profiles added to platform simultaneously
- Agencies onboard entire client rosters, dramatically accelerating talent-side numbers
- Agency outreach to brands on behalf of talent creates brand user discovery
- Target: Each agency signing adds average 18 talent profiles (based on typical agency roster)
- 100 agency users x 18 talent profiles = 1,800 talent users from agency channel alone

- Every manager signup should prompt talent invitation (target 60% invitation rate)
- Every talent signup should prompt manager invitation (target 20% invitation rate)
- Manager + talent bundle creates cohort retention (harder to churn when two accounts are linked)
- Target: 400 of 1,000 manager users bring at least 1 talent user with them = 400 bonus talent acquisitions

**Flywheel Mechanism 5: Network Effect on AI Match Engine**

- More users of all types = more data = better AI recommendations
- Better AI recommendations = higher deal conversion = better case studies
- Better case studies = easier sales = more users
- This is the compounding mechanism that makes the platform harder to displace at scale
