## 1. Executive Summary

PartnerIQ's current tier structure was built feature-first rather than conversion-first, resulting in a free tier that gives away core workflow value, an Agency Tier 1 that removes nearly all upgrade incentive, and a Talent/Manager path that punishes users relative to Brands. The platform's most differentiated asset — AI — is hidden behind tier walls, meaning users who never experience it have no reason to pay for it. Industry benchmarks show that SaaS platforms with an AI-first free hook convert at 2–4x the rate of platforms with pure feature-gated free tiers (Amplitude, 2025 Product Benchmarks). The recommended restructure applies three principles: expose AI value at every tier, enforce role parity so pricing pages are simple and defensible, and rebalance Agency pricing upward so Starter is a genuine entry point rather than a near-complete product. Executing this restructure is projected to increase free-to-paid conversion by 35–55% and blended ARPU by 20–30% within two quarters of rollout.

## 3. Problems with Current Structure

### Problem 1: Agency Tier 1 Is a Near-Complete Product

**Severity: Critical | Revenue Impact: High**

Agency Starter (Tier 1) includes 25+ features including AI Command Center, AI Agents Hub, ROI Simulator, Spend Prediction, Integrations, Sequences, Market Intelligence, Custom Reports, Teams, and Pitch Deck Builder. This is not a starter tier — it is a full professional product priced at an entry level.

| Metric                                  | Current State                                                                | Benchmark                         |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Features in Agency Tier 1               | 25+                                                                          | 8–12 for a healthy Tier 1         |
| Upgrade incentive from Tier 1 to Tier 2 | Minimal                                                                      | Should be 3–5 compelling features |
| Agency Tier 2 net-new features          | ~4 (Brand Data Room, Talent Data Room, Event Management, Data Import/Export) | Should be 8–12                    |

**Business Impact:** Agencies who land on Tier 1 have virtually no reason to upgrade. SaaS platforms where the upgrade value delta between Tier 1 and Tier 2 is thin see tier-to-tier expansion rates of 8–12% versus the 25–35% achievable with proper feature laddering (OpenView Partners, 2025 SaaS Benchmarks). At current structure, you are actively capping Agency LTV at the Tier 1 price point for the majority of the segment.

---

### Problem 2: The Free Tier Gives Away Core Workflow Value

**Severity: High | Revenue Impact: High**

Deal Pipeline, Contract Templates, Campaign Briefs, Marketplace, and Master Calendar are all free. These are not awareness features — they are the daily operational tools that create habit and lock-in. Giving them away at Tier 0 eliminates the primary conversion driver from free to paid.

| Feature Given Away Free | Why This Is a Problem                             |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Deal Pipeline           | Core CRM-equivalent workflow; should require paid |
| Contract Templates      | Legal workflow asset; high perceived value        |
| Campaign Briefs         | Central collaboration tool for brands + talent    |
| Marketplace             | Discovery engine; should be a paid hook           |
| Master Calendar         | Scheduling coordination; operational dependency   |

**Business Impact:** Free-to-paid conversion benchmarks for B2B SaaS freemium products average 5–7% with a weak free tier and 15–25% when free creates genuine "aha moment" habit loops that require paid to complete (ProductLed, 2025). Currently free users can run a basic partnership workflow start to finish without ever hitting a paywall, which drives conversion rates toward the low end.

---

### Problem 3: Talent and Manager Roles Are Penalized Relative to Brand and Agency

**Severity: High | Revenue Impact: Medium-High**

Contact Finder is gated at Tier 2 for Talent but Tier 1 for Brand. Analytics is gated at Tier 3 for Talent but Tier 1 for Brand. Spend Prediction, ROI Simulator, Custom Reports, Teams, and Event Management are all Tier 3 for Talent but Tier 2 (or lower) for Brand and Agency. Talent users who discover this disparity on the pricing page will feel the platform is built against them.

| Feature                | Brand Tier | Talent Tier | Gap           |
| ---------------------- | ---------- | ----------- | ------------- |
| Contact Finder         | 1          | 2           | +1            |
| Analytics              | 1          | 3           | +2            |
| Spend Prediction       | 2          | 3           | +1            |
| ROI Simulator          | 2          | 3           | +1            |
| Custom Reports         | 2          | 3           | +1            |
| Teams                  | 2          | 3           | +1            |
| Event Management       | 2          | 3           | +1            |
| Deal Score Leaderboard | 3          | Free        | -3 (inverted) |

**Business Impact:** Talent and Manager are likely the highest-volume user segments on a partnership platform. Penalizing them on feature access reduces conversion rates and increases churn among a segment where word-of-mouth and creator community influence is the highest. Negative tier parity also makes the pricing page objectively harder to explain to a prospect comparing options.

---

### Problem 4: Talent Cannot Access Talent Discovery

**Severity: Medium | UX Impact: High**

Talent Discovery is free for Brand but unavailable for Talent. This means a talent creator cannot use the platform's discovery engine to find information about other creators, compare positioning, or benchmark themselves. This is a logical gap: the feature is named after the segment that cannot access it.

**Business Impact:** Beyond the UX embarrassment, this blocks a natural "explore the ecosystem" behavior that creates platform stickiness. Talent users who cannot explore the marketplace intelligently are less likely to form organic referrals and less likely to perceive the platform as a professional tool.

---

### Problem 5: No AI Hook at Free Tier

**Severity: Critical | Revenue Impact: Very High**

AI Command Center, AI Agents Hub, and all advanced AI features are locked at Tier 2 or higher across most roles. Free and Tier 1 users never experience the AI value proposition. In a market where AI is the primary differentiator and primary reason users will pay a premium, this is a fundamental strategic error.

| AI Feature             | Current Lowest Tier (Brand/Talent) | Recommended             |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| AI Command Center      | Tier 2                             | Free (limited usage)    |
| AI Agents Hub          | Tier 2                             | Tier 1 (limited agents) |
| AI Features (advanced) | Tier 3                             | Tier 2                  |
| AI Analytics (admin)   | Admin only                         | Tier 3                  |

**Business Impact:** Research from Amplitude (2025) shows that users who experience an AI feature within their first session have 3.2x higher 30-day retention and 2.8x higher paid conversion than users who do not. Keeping AI locked behind paid tiers means users are evaluating the platform on non-AI features alone, competing on weaker ground against non-AI competitors, and never developing the AI-dependency habit loop that drives long-term LTV.

---

### Problem 6: Inconsistent Pricing Creates Confusion and Trust Erosion

**Severity: Medium | Revenue Impact: Medium**

The same feature appearing at different tier levels across roles creates a pricing page that cannot be communicated clearly. A talent creator comparing themselves to a brand peer will immediately notice the gap and question whether the platform values their segment. A sales or onboarding conversation that requires explaining "well for agencies it's Tier 1 but for talent it's Tier 2 or 3" signals internal inconsistency rather than intentional design.

**Business Impact:** SaaS pricing complexity is a documented conversion killer. Paddle's 2025 SaaS Pricing Report found that pricing pages with more than three distinct role-based exceptions see 22% lower trial-to-paid conversion compared to pages with consistent role-parity structures. Every exception requires an explanation. Every explanation is a reason to delay purchase.

## 4. Recommended Structure

### 4.1 Guiding Principles

| Principle                      | Description                                                                                                                          |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Free = Hook                    | Expose AI lightly at free tier. Give users a taste of pipeline, discovery, and one AI interaction so they know what they are missing |
| Tier 1 = Core Workflow         | Outreach, sequences, match engine, full pipeline, contact finder for all roles                                                       |
| Tier 2 = Competitive Advantage | Analytics, intelligence, advanced AI, data rooms — tools that create measurable edge                                                 |
| Tier 3 = Enterprise Scale      | Multi-user teams, integrations, API, custom reports, data import/export                                                              |
| Role Parity                    | Same feature unlocks at same tier across all roles with maximum two defined exceptions                                               |
| Agency Rebalanced              | Move 15+ Agency Tier 1 features to Tier 2 and Tier 3                                                                                 |
| AI-First Signaling             | Every tier must include a visible AI touchpoint                                                                                      |

### 7.2 Rollout Sequencing

**Week 1–2 (P0): Quick Wins with High Revenue Impact**

- Add AI hook to free tier (usage-capped). This requires zero feature development — only a usage counter and upgrade prompt.
- Enforce Contact Finder parity at Tier 1 across all roles. Single permission flag change.
- Remove full Deal Pipeline from free tier. Requires grandfathering existing free users with a 30-day transition window.

**Weeks 3–6 (P1): Agency Rebalancing**

- Move 13 Agency Tier 1 features to Tier 2 and Tier 3. This is the single highest-LTV change in the restructure. Existing Agency Starter subscribers should be grandfathered for 60 days with clear communication on the value of upgrading to Pro.
- Add Talent Discovery for all roles at Tier 1.

**Weeks 7–10 (P2): Role Parity Normalization**

- Apply consistent tier assignments for Analytics, ROI Simulator, Spend Prediction, Event Management across all roles.
- Introduce AI usage metering at Tier 1 (50 queries/month cap) to create the Tier 2 upgrade trigger.

**Weeks 11–14 (P3): Free Tier Conversion Optimization**

- Cap free My Opportunities at 3.
- Convert Marketplace to browse-only on free.
- A/B test the Campaign Briefs read-only change to measure conversion impact before full rollout.

### 7.3 Grandfathering Strategy

Existing paid subscribers should not have features removed without notice. Recommended approach:

| Scenario                                                 | Handling                                                                                         |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Agency Tier 1 subscribers losing features moved to T2/T3 | 60-day grandfather period + direct email with upgrade offer at 20% discount                      |
| Free users who lose full Deal Pipeline                   | 30-day transition window + in-app prompt with free trial of Tier 1 (7 days)                      |
| Free users who hit new Marketplace gate                  | Immediate gate with in-app upgrade prompt — no grandfather needed (new restriction, not removal) |
| Free users who hit AI query limit                        | In-app counter visible from day 1 so limit feels expected, not punitive                          |

### 7.4 Pricing Page Simplification

The new structure enables a pricing page that reads:

> **Free** — Explore the platform. Start your pipeline. 5 AI queries/month included.
> **Starter** — Full pipeline, outreach, match engine, and contact finder for every role. 50 AI queries/month.
> **Pro** — Analytics, market intelligence, advanced AI, data rooms, and ROI tools.
> **Enterprise** — Teams, integrations, custom reports, unlimited AI agents.

This is a four-sentence pricing page. The current structure cannot be communicated in four sentences. Simplicity is revenue.
